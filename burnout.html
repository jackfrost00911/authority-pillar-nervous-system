Burnout & The Nervous System

How chronic sympathetic load leads to the "High-Functioning Freeze" response.

We need to talk about the "I'm Fine" lie.

You know the one. Someone asks how you are, and you say "I'm good, just busy!" while internally you feel like a laptop that has 47 tabs open and the cooling fan is screaming.

Then, one random Tuesday, you spill a glass of water and just... stare at it. You don't clean it up. You just sit on the floor and think about moving to a goat farm in Peru. That isn't "laziness." That is biology.

The Circuit Breaker

Burnout is not an emotion. It is a safety mechanism. If you stay in Fight-or-Flight too long, your body slams on the emergency brake to prevent engine failure. This is Dorsal Vagal Shutdown.

Why Vacations Usually Fail

Have you ever gone on a vacation, expecting to relax, but instead you just felt numb? Or got sick immediately?

That is because you removed the adrenaline that was holding you together. If your internal switch is stuck in "Danger" mode, sitting on a beach just makes you an anxious person in a swimsuit.

How to Actually Recover

Recovery is not about "doing nothing." Recovery is active regulation.

  • Mechanical Decompression: Releasing the physical tension in the neck and diaphragm.
  • Low-Stakes Movement: Walking without a podcast. Moving without a goal.
  • Completing the Stress Cycle: Using the "Panic Eject" tools to flush the cortisol buildup.
